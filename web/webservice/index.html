NapEasy: Neat Automated Publication highlighting made EASY
The NapEasy web server automatically highlights important sentences in publications available through the PubMed Central (PMC) Open Access dataset. The highlights are concise and aim to represent the main characteristics of the study described in the publication. While the workflow is similar to generating a summary, the sentences highlighted may not necessarily be the best to represent the content of the paper as the aim is to select those sentences, describing best the study. NapEasy is designed to help facilitate and speedup the curation processes for faster knowledge acquisition.

How to use [-]
The only input NapEasy needs is a list of comma separated PMCIDs. Note that PMIDs (PubMed identifiers) are different to PMCIDs. Should you only have PMIDs to hand, an easy conversion tool is available online.

There are two options to use NapEasy:

    1) If you happen to have PMC IDs of papers you want to highlight, just click the "Use PMC IDs" tab below, enter the relevant PMC IDs and submit.
    2) Otherwise, just simply use the "Search PMC" functionality below to look up and select papers based on keyword search; the selected papers can then immediately be submitted for highlighting.
In its current implementation, the service will not provide highlight results on the fly. Instead, a unique job id and URI for the job will be generated on submission (e.g., sample job URI), which will allow you to check the result later (so, please make a note of it if you don't want to provide an email address). If you do provide your email address during the submission process, you will receive a notification email from napeasy.noreply@gmail.com when your highlighting job has finished. (Normally, a one paper job finishes within 20-30 minutes; more than one paper will take longer.) The highlighted sentences can then be downloaded in three different formats (XML, JSON, and plain text) for further analysis.
